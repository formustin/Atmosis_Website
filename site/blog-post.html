Upholstery Fabric Guide!

Interior design
14
Aug 15, 2020
byDezingrid

I’ve always loved great upholstery, and think that a great sofa is one of the most important pieces of furniture in your home. – Bobby Berk.

STAY UPBEAT WITH UPHOLSTERY FABRIC

Have you ever noticed the soft, padded textile covering on furniture such as armchairs and sofas?

This is upholstery fabric which originated in the Middle Ages and has become a niche industry today. Upholstering is also about stuffing furniture with padding, webbing, springs and foam before layering with suitable fabric.

While choosing or manufacturing furniture, you can decide on the fabric based on the wall colouring and other furnishings in your room. The fabric for upholstery can also be used to recover old furniture.

Choosing the Best Upholstery Fabric for Your Sofa, Chairs, or Headboard

Choosing the right fabric should be based on your living area, weather conditions and whether you have pets living with you or not. Urban areas with heavy traffic require durable fabrics which are long-lasting. Fibers used could be cotton, wool, linen, polyester, nylon, olefin, leather, acetate, silk or rayon. Each fabric has its own merits and demerits. With a host of fabrics that are visually appealing as well as competing for durability, there are a few basic features to consider to select the right fabric for your living or office spaces.

The Best Upholstery Fabrics for Homes with Kids and Pets

Pets can be emotionally fulfilling and be wonderful companions but can do untold damage with their claws, teeth and paws. The good news is that today we have pet-friendly fabrics that can withstand such vicious attacks to some extent. Leather is stain, odour and puncture resistant, and fur is not likely to cling on to the fabric. Olefin is another good option. Fabrics such as silk, linen and cotton are best avoided. Lapping in Luxury with Paradigm Patterns in Upholstery

Upholstery fabric provides you the freedom to experiment with range of colours and materials in adding sophistication and character to your interiors. They are applicable for curtains, beds, carpets and furniture, and a host of other furnishings. Every upholstery fabric type has its own unique charm and adequate knowledge about different upholstery fabric can gear you up to arrive at a practical and sensible decision quickly and easily. Here are a few for your reference.

Leather


Leather is preferred widely for its comfort and elegance, especially in living rooms and office spaces.


Price Range : Rs.100 to 300 per meter
Pros:

  • It does not absorb spills and is odour-resistant.
  • Stains can be removed with soap and water, or a cleaning solution recommended by the manufacturer.

    • Cons:
    • It can be damaged with sharp objects.
    • It is expensive.
    • It is unsuitable for warm areas.


    Polyester

    Polyester is another popular upholstery fabric. It is available in many colours, and blend in with your room’s theme.

    Price Range : Rs.20 to 100 per meter

    Pros:

    • It is a soft fabric that is suitable to all climatic conditions.
    • Cleaning procedure is simple and drying is quick.
    • It is stain- and abrasion-resistant.

    Cons:

  • It can tear if exposed to extreme temperatures.
  • It is likely to stretch over time.
  • It is inflammable and it is risky to smoke near it.

Olefin

It is a petroleum-based synthetic fiber and is ideal for rough use as it is quite durable and long-lasting.

Price Range : Rs.50 to 100 per meter

Pros:

  • It is water-, fire-, and chemical-resistant.
  • Water-based stains can be cleaned easily.
  • It is also resistant to fading.

Cons:

  • Olefin is vulnerable to oil, light, and heat.
  • It has a limited variety and therefore not preferable for indoors.
  • Too much exposure to sunlight can make olefin brittle.

    • Care and Maintenance

    In addition to revitalising our living spaces with a fabric that will blend in with our existing interior decor, selecting a fabric that is enduring and immune to our living conditions can pose a challenge. Therefore, it is helpful to be equipped with the know of a product’s durability and maintenance.

    Fabric Care

    Once the fabric is installed, washing is out of the question. Therefore, it is advisable to choose stain-repellent fabrics for frequently used products. Spot cleaning for removing stains is suggested as per manufacturer’s instructions. Using a fabric protectant spray is highly recommended.

    Durability

    The durability rating is based on the manufacturer-administered double rub test, which is a back and forth motion that mimics the wear and tear of someone sitting on an upholstered seat over time. The higher the number of double rubs, (for example 100,000 vs. 10,000) the better the indicator of quality and durability. In addition to higher double rubs, look for upholstery fabrics treated for performance, like stain-, UV-, and water resistant fabrics. Use delicate fabrics for decorative performances, which will not be subjected to everyday wear and tear.

    FORMALLY ASKED QUESTIONS

    1. What will be the ideal colour choice?

    Beige is excellent in hiding dirt and stains and is a neutral colour that will blend with any background. Consider lighter colours to create an airy, inviting mood in a space.

    2. Can I re-upholster or buy new?

    The structural quality and sturdiness of a furniture has to be taken into consideration before opting for re-upholstering existing furniture. Get to know the quality and warranty of the fabric before purchasing.

    3. How long does it take to reupholster a piece of furniture?

    It can take anywhere between 5 and 15 days and is based on how complex the job is.

    4. Why is it important to seek professional help to clean furniture fabric?

    With time, there will be accumulation of dirt and dust trapped in the fabric which might lead to respiratory ailments in children and the elderly. Professional clean can do a more thorough job than what can be attempted at home.
