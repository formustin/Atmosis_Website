All You Need to Know about Rugs

Interior design
14
Aug 14, 2020
byDezingrid

Rugs make a hard surface look better and soften it up. A good rug completes the sections of a room. Ron Testa

Have you ever wondered how rugs came into being?

Many centuries ago, shepherds began knotting wool into heavy woven cloth. This heavy cloth safeguarded them from the elements.
Whether the first rugs were made in the near East or Siberia is not clear, but over the centuries rug making, a profession born out of a necessity, transformed into a lucrative art form for commercial purposes.
The patterns, vibrant colors and many knots per square inch produced beautiful rugs. Each artisan showed amazing creative potential in designing, dying and producing these prized rugs.

Tufted

Loops of yarn or wool are inserted into a canvas that is held on a frame using a hand-operated tool. The loops are then sheared to create a smooth surface. Once there is a thick piling of the wool, it is removed from the frame and the canvas is glued to the back of the woolen surface.

Knotted Rug

This type of rug is created by knotting the fibers of the rug and is quite a laborious process.

Flat Woven

The process is similar to that of knotted rugs. However, unlike knotted rugs where each knot is cut after creation, here the initial thread is retained to create more knots. This is much easier to make than knotted rugs.

Hooked

Known for their elaborate designs, these rugs can be made using crochet needles by inserting strips of wool through a burlap or linen frame.

Braided

These rugs are made by braiding long strips of cloth and stitching them together.


STYLES OF RUGS


Traditional Rugs

Bohemian Rugs

Coastal

Industrial

Tropical

Traditional Rugs

Traditional rugs usually have floral and other intricate patterns with borders and central medallions in geometric shapes.

Bohemian Rugs

Bohemian styles come in a combination of intricate patterns of vibrant or pastel colours, which provide a playful yet a romantic appeal without being gaudy.

Coastal

Rugs made of sisal, jute or bamboo make up a coastal décor due to their ability to provide a cool, beach tone. These rugs do not make use of flashy colors or textures. They are usually a blend of white and blue or blue-green shades.

Industrial

These are rugs designed with a utilitarian point of view for use in industries and have little or no embellishment. These are usually made of nylon or polypropylene due to their resistance to wear and tear.

Tropical

A tropical setting with palm trees, plants and fruits, tropical rugs sport bold colours like cypress green, coral, orange, turquoise, and red which will bring in an aura of paradise into your bedroom, living room, or outdoor patio.

FEATURES OF RUGS

Once you have decided on the type, material, style and pattern of a rug, you will want to take a closer look at any additional special features the rug may have.

NON-SLIP BACKING

Non-slip backings are constructed from materials that create lots of friction with the surface underneath them. A non-slip backing is a popular feature in many different rugs, especially kitchen and bath rugs

LOOR HEATING SAFE

If your home or apartment has radiant heating, this feature will be critical for you. Depending on the material a rug is created from, excessive heat from below the rug could pose serious problems or safety concerns.

ECO-FRIENDLY

Eco-friendly production ensures that regardless of how the rug is constructed, it is done so in a manner that does not pose harm to the environment.

REVERSIBLE

Reversible rugs add an extra layer of versatility and they can double your style options with a rug.

RUG CLEANING AND CARE

Rug cleaning and care is very important and can be done by professionals or yourself. You don’t need to regularly clean rugs on a weekly or monthly basis. Instead, you should clean them when they are visibly dirty or have an odour.

KEEP YOUR TOOLS AND SUPPLIES CLOSE

  • Rug shampoo
  • Bucket
  • Water
  • Sponge

REMOVE DIRT AND DEBRIS

You should vacuum clean on both sides of the rug and make sure every bit of debris is gone.

MIX YOUR CLEANSER

You can use a cleaning solution or rug shampoo for cleaning. You can also use a mild dishwashing detergent mixed in a bucket with warm mater.

DO A COLOR TEST

It is important to make sure the cleaner does not cause the colours to run. Test the solution on the corner of the rug to make sure it is colorfast.

WASH THE RUG

The next step is to use a sponge or brush and work the cleaning solution into a lather on the rug. Let the cleaner soak on the rug for a few minutes before you start rinsing.

RINSE THE RUG

Rinse the soap out of the rug using a bucket of water.

REMOVE EXCESS WATER

Once you rinse the rug, you may want to get rid of the excess water.

LET THE RUG DRY

The next step, is to let the rug dry out. Ensure that both sides are dry. Fans can help speed up the process.

VACUUM

The final step is to vacuum and dry the rug completely. Now, you’re finished and can enjoy your freshly cleaned carpet.
